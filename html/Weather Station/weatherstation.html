Weather Station

This is a project by Robert and Jake. We are building a weather system that will be able to gauge the air temperature, humidity, pressure, wind speed, wind direction, and rain fall. Our weather system will be mostly made of 3d printed parts.

    Parts:
    • A Raspberry Pi with built-in wireless connectivity or has with a WiFi dongle
    • A BME280 pressure, temperature, and humidity sensor
    • Two 4.7 KOhm resistors
    • Some 5mm-pitch PCB mount screw terminal blocks
    • A breadboard, some jumper wires
    • An anemometer, wind vane, and rain gauge (We need to build these from 3d printed parts.
    • Two RJ11 breakout boards
    • An MCP3008 analog-to-digital converter integrated circuit
    • Weatherproof enclosures
    • Rain gauge sensor.
    • Solid core wire (22 AWG)
    • An Adafruit Perma-Proto HAT for Pi Mini Kit
    • A 16-pin DIL/DIP IC Socket
    • Two 2-pin male headers
    • General tools: side-cutters, wire strippers, screwdrivers, etc
    • Insulating tape
    • Access to a 3D printer
